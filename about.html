Computography Lab

About us

Computography Lab exists to bring transparency and creativity to the control and operation of today's cameras.

The founding story

A photographer's problem, a technologist's solution

Computography Lab was founded by a lifelong photographer and career-long technology leader whose personal creative journey led directly to this project — and whose frustrations are almost certainly familiar to serious Olympus and OM SYSTEM shooters everywhere.

The cameras have evolved from being devices to being systems — but the tools for configuring and controlling them haven't kept pace.

For decades, the most technically ambitious photography has involved careful manipulation of exposure control, lighting, superimposition, white balance, sequential shots, and video to produce compositions that play with the viewer's perception of light, time, space, and motion. As cameras have become more powerful, more of this work can happen in-camera rather than in post — which is exciting. But the growth of features has come at a cost.

The manual for one recent Lumix body runs to more than 900 pages. The manual for the OM-1 Mark II tops 600. Finding, understanding, and correctly setting the right controls for a specific creative intent has become genuinely difficult. The on-camera custom settings functionality — C1, C2, C3 — was designed for a simpler era and hasn't scaled with the complexity of what these cameras can now do.

OlyProbe is the first step toward solving this. It reads every control available on a connected camera, captures its current state, and makes that configuration portable, shareable, and reusable. Think of it as C1 through C-infinity — an unlimited digital configuration memory for your camera that lives outside the camera.

OlyComp builds on this foundation, separating the camera from its controls entirely and enabling real-time remote configuration, live preview, and scripted computational shooting sequences that the camera's own firmware cannot execute.

Our language

What we mean by Computography

We use a few terms that are specific to the Computography Lab community. Here is what they mean.

Computography™

A portmanteau of Computational Photography. Analog computational photography techniques have been used for decades, but today's highly intelligent cameras — particularly those from OM SYSTEM — are enabling in-camera digital controls and computational features that go far beyond what was previously possible. These cameras can also be configured and controlled remotely from intelligent devices for both standard and computational functions, enabling real-time preview of configuration changes and scripted sequential shooting for sophisticated time-lapse, bracketing, in-camera compositing, and more. Computography marries the powerful capabilities of advanced cameras with the flexibility and creativity of remote configuration and control.

High-intent photography

Photography that uses calculation, planning, and orchestrated execution to achieve a specific image goal. It includes fashion, studio, architecture, nature, fine art, and other disciplines where the final image is the result of deliberate technical and creative decisions made before, during, and after the shutter fires. High-intent photographers are the primary audience for Computography Lab's tools.

Setting Cheats

A complete snapshot of a camera's configuration at a specific moment — every control, every value — captured by OlyProbe and saved for reuse. A Setting Cheat can be applied to the same camera later to instantly restore a known-good configuration, or shared with the community for others to use as a starting point for their own work. The name is deliberate: these are shortcuts that let you skip the 600-page manual.

What we are building

The Computography Lab roadmap

We are building a suite of tools for the OM SYSTEM and Olympus community. Here is where things stand.

✓
Live now

OlyProbe — camera capability interrogation

A free tool that connects to any compatible Olympus or OM SYSTEM camera via USB, reads every available control and its permitted values, and produces a complete capability map. The foundation for everything that follows.

✓
Live now

Setting Cheats community library

A subscriber community where photographers share complete camera configurations — including example images and EXIF metadata — for specific shooting scenarios. Browse, download as PDF, or apply directly to a connected camera.

→
In development

OlyComp — creative remote control

A remote control application for OM SYSTEM cameras that handles standard controls and enables computational shooting sequences not available in-camera. Live preview with real-time configuration adjustment is a core goal.

○
Planned

Cross-camera capability analysis

A tool for comparing the full capability sets of different camera models side by side — useful for camera shoppers, multi-camera shooters, and anyone trying to understand what a firmware update has changed.

○
Planned

Broader camera coverage

OlyProbe currently focuses on Olympus and OM SYSTEM bodies. Expanding coverage to additional camera models and manufacturers is on the horizon as the community and capability library grow.

Get involved

How to contribute

Computography Lab is a small operation with large ambitions. There are two ways to contribute — with data and with code — and both matter equally.

Contribute camera data

Download OlyProbe, connect your Olympus or OM SYSTEM camera, and submit your camera's capability data. Every submission expands the community library and improves cross-camera analysis for everyone. No technical knowledge required.

Contribute Setting Cheats

Subscribe and share your camera configurations with the community. A great Setting Cheat — with a compelling example image — helps other photographers discover what their cameras can do and inspires new creative approaches.
